Safe Publications Library

The Archive Foundation publishes sanitized research on infohazards and information security. These publications have been reviewed and cleared to ensure they contain no harmful content while still providing valuable knowledge to researchers and practitioners.

JOURNAL PUBLICATIONS
Cognitive Resilience: Techniques for Information Hazard Mitigation
Chen, E., Hammond, M., et al.
Journal of Information Security, Vol. 24, No. 3 (2000)
This paper presents validated techniques for building cognitive resilience against low-to-moderate level information hazards. Protocols described have been tested in controlled laboratory settings and field studies with volunteers. Download PDF (450KB)
Dimensional Anomalies and Perceptual Defense Mechanisms NEW
Thompson, J., Rodriguez, A., et al.
Applied Cognitive Sciences, Vol. 12, No. 1 (2001)
Analysis of public response to the 1996 Taos Incident, with emphasis on psychological defense mechanisms that prevent harmful processing of anomalous perceptual input. Download PDF (620KB)
Automated Detection of Hazardous Information Patterns
Williams, S., Johnson, P., et al.
IEEE Transactions on Information Theory, Vol. 45, No. 8 (1999)
Technical overview of pattern-matching algorithms developed for early detection of potentially hazardous information structures in digital content. Implementation specifications have been redacted. Download PDF (380KB)
Historical Analysis of Documented Infohazard Incidents: 1950-1995
Singh, R., Anderson, C., et al.
Archive Foundation Quarterly, Vol. 18, No. 2 (1998)
Statistical analysis of documented infohazard incidents during the pre-Hypernet era, with sanitized case studies and trend analysis. Download PDF (540KB)
BOOKS AND MONOGRAPHS
Information Hazards: A Comprehensive Framework
Roberts, E. & Martinez, L.
Archive Foundation Press (1997)
The definitive text on information hazard classification, containment, and mitigation. Used as the primary reference for training programs worldwide. Available in hardcover only (not available for download). Order from Bookstore
Protecting Your Mind: A Citizen's Guide to Information Safety
Taylor, H.
Public Information Series, Archive Foundation Press (2000)
Written for the general public, this accessible guide explains basic concepts of information security and provides practical advice for everyday situations. Download PDF (780KB)
Digital Containment: Information Security in the Hypernet Age
Keller, M. & Wong, J.
Archive Foundation Press (1999)
Technical monograph on digital containment strategies for information hazards in networked environments. Contains technical specifications for secure systems design. Download PDF (820KB)
TECHNICAL REPORTS
Sanitized Report: The Taos Incident (1996)
Archive Foundation Special Investigation Team
Public Release Version 2.3 (1999)
Official sanitized report on the dimensional anomaly that appeared near Taos, New Mexico in August 1996. Contains witness accounts and analysis of public response. Download PDF (650KB)
Infohazard Protection for Educational Institutions
Education Safety Committee
Technical Report #AF-EDU-121 (2000)
Guidelines for schools, colleges, and libraries on implementing information safety protocols to protect students and the public. Download PDF (420KB)
Annual Risk Assessment: Emerging Information Hazards
Risk Analysis Division
Technical Report #AF-RISK-047 (2000)
Analysis of emerging information hazard categories and risk factors in the digital age. Includes sanitized trend data and preventive recommendations. Download PDF (580KB)
