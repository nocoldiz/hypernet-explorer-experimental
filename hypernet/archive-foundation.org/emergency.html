Emergency Protocols

These protocols are designed for individuals who believe they may have been exposed to infohazardous material. Time is critical. Follow these steps immediately if you experience unusual cognitive effects after encountering suspicious information.

IMMEDIATE RESPONSE PROCEDURES
  1. Stop interacting with the suspected hazardous material immediately
  2. Close any Hypernet browser windows or documents containing the information
  3. Focus on a simple, repetitive task like counting or breathing exercises
  4. Do NOT attempt to recall or describe the hazardous information to others
  5. Call the Foundation Emergency Line
1-800-INFO-911
RECOGNIZING EXPOSURE SYMPTOMS

Common symptoms of infohazard exposure may include but are not limited to:

  • Unusual thought patterns or obsessive focus on specific concepts
  • Perception distortions (visual, auditory, or temporal)
  • Strong emotional responses incongruent with stimuli
  • Sudden onset of headache or disorientation
  • Compulsion to share specific information
  • Memory gaps or difficulty concentrating

If you experience any of these symptoms, proceed with the immediate response procedures above.

ASSISTING EXPOSED INDIVIDUALS

If you witness someone showing signs of infohazard exposure:

  1. Remain calm and speak in a clear, reassuring voice
  2. Guide them away from the source of exposure
  3. Do NOT ask them to describe what they saw or read
  4. Encourage them to focus on immediate physical surroundings
  5. Call the emergency line and follow dispatcher instructions

Foundation response teams are equipped with cognitive decontamination tools and are available 24/7 in most major cities.
