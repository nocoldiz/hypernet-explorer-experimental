Civilian Training Programs

The Archive Foundation offers several training programs to help civilians protect themselves and their communities from infohazardous threats. These courses are designed to build cognitive resilience and awareness without exposing participants to actual harmful information.

All training programs are certified by the International Information Safety Board (IISB)
AVAILABLE COURSES
Infohazard Awareness Basics
Level: Beginner | Duration: 2 hours
Introduction to infohazard concepts, recognition of warning signs, and basic avoidance strategies. Suitable for all adults and teenagers (14+).
Digital Safety: Hypernet Navigation
Level: Beginner | Duration: 3 hours
Specific techniques for safe browsing, recognition of potentially harmful Hypernet content, and proper use of protective software including cognitive filters.
Cognitive Resilience Training
Level: Intermediate | Duration: Full day
Mental exercises to strengthen resistance against minor infohazards, including attention control and recovery techniques.
Emergency Response for Families
Level: Beginner | Duration: 4 hours
Practical guidance for parents and caregivers on protecting children from infohazards and responding to accidental exposure incidents.
Community First Responder
Level: Advanced | Duration: Weekend course
In-depth training for designated community representatives who serve as first points of contact during infohazard incidents. Includes certification.
Dimensional Anomaly Awareness NEW
Level: Intermediate | Duration: 4 hours
Special course developed following the 1996 Taos Incident. Focuses on recognition of spatial/temporal distortions and appropriate safety measures.
REGISTRATION INFORMATION

To register for any of our training programs:

  • Call our training line: 1-800-INFO-EDU
  • Visit your local Archive Foundation office
  • Complete our online registration form

Most civilian courses are offered free of charge as a public service. Advanced and specialized courses may have nominal fees to cover materials.
