Hypercapitalist Collective

Last updated: December 15, 2001 | 7 min read

The Hypercapitalist Collective is a powerful transnational organization that emerged following the Y2K Collapse and the Squishing of 1997. Operating as both a corporate conglomerate and a socio-economic movement, the Collective has become one of the most influential forces in the post-Squishing world, utilizing velocimancy and chronoeconomic manipulation to maintain their dominance.

Overview

The Hypercapitalist Collective represents the evolution of corporate power in a world where the traditional rules of physics and economics have been fundamentally altered. Unlike conventional corporations, the Collective operates on principles of kinetic capitalism, where velocity and acceleration are not just metaphors but literal sources of economic power. Members of the Collective have undergone extensive biomodification to achieve superhuman speed and endurance, allowing them to exploit the Rule of 80 that governs the post-Squishing world.

Formation and Early History

The Collective's origins can be traced to the immediate aftermath of the Y2K Collapse, when traditional economic systems worldwide faced unprecedented disruption. A consortium of tech entrepreneurs, financial analysts, and what would later be identified as early velocimancers recognized that the emerging reality required entirely new approaches to wealth generation and market manipulation.

The formal establishment occurred in early 1999, when several major corporations began pooling resources to develop what they termed "acceleration-based profit models." The discovery that sustained high-velocity movement could circumvent the temporal anomalies affecting slower-moving entities provided the foundation for their revolutionary business practices.

Ideology and Philosophy

The Collective's core philosophy centers around the concept of "Perpetual Motion Capitalism," the belief that economic stagnation is not merely inefficient but physically dangerous in the post-Squishing world. Their ideology emphasizes:

  • Velocity as Currency: The faster an entity moves, the more valuable it becomes
  • Temporal Arbitrage: Exploiting time differentials for profit maximization
  • Biocommercial Enhancement: Modifying human physiology to optimize market performance
  • Dimensional Resource Exploitation: Mining opportunities across multiple reality layers
"In a world where standing still means literal temporal imprisonment, only through constant acceleration can true freedom—and profit—be achieved." - Core Tenet of the Collective

Organizational Structure

The Collective operates through a fluid hierarchy known as the "Profit Cascade," where positions are determined not by traditional corporate metrics but by demonstrated velocity capabilities and chronoeconomic output. The structure includes:

Velocity Lords

The highest tier, consisting of individuals capable of maintaining speeds exceeding 200 km/h while conducting complex financial transactions. These enhanced beings serve as the Collective's primary decision-makers and strategic coordinators.

Acceleration Managers

Middle management enhanced for sustained high-speed operations, typically maintaining 120-180 km/h during business operations. They oversee regional profit-zones and coordinate between Velocity Lords and lower tiers.

Momentum Workers

The foundational workforce, modified to operate at minimum velocities of 80 km/h. While less enhanced than their superiors, they form the backbone of the Collective's operations across various dimensional markets.

Velocimantic Technology

The Collective's technological advantages stem from their mastery of velocimancy—the fusion of kinetic energy manipulation with financial algorithms. Key innovations include:

  • Kineto-Financial Interfaces: Trading terminals that respond to the user's speed and acceleration
  • Chronodilution Fields: Technology that slows time in localized areas for extended decision-making
  • Momentum Amplifiers: Devices that convert physical motion into market manipulation energy
  • Profit-Stream Accelerators: Infrastructure that increases revenue flow proportional to operational velocity

Operations and Influence

The Collective's influence extends across multiple sectors of the post-Squishing economy. Their primary operations include:

Dimensional Market Manipulation

Exploiting price differentials between reality layers, the Collective has established trading posts in various dimensional fragments accessible through Hypernet connections. This allows them to buy low in one reality and sell high in another.

Temporal Commodity Trading

The Collective has pioneered the commodification of time itself, buying and selling temporal experiences, memories, and even future possibilities. Their "Time Banks" allow clients to deposit unused moments and withdraw them with interest.

Infrastructure Development

Working closely with Esoteric Heavy Industries, the Collective develops high-speed transportation networks and velocity-optimized commercial zones designed to maintain their competitive advantages.

Relations with Other Factions

The Collective maintains complex relationships with other major post-Squishing organizations:

  • The Gods: Alliance based on mutual benefit—the Collective provides resources while the Gods offer protection from dimensional instabilities
  • Guild of Wizards: Neutral relationship, with occasional technology exchanges
  • Archive Foundation: Ongoing conflict over information control and transparency requirements
  • Goblins: Hostile, due to competing resource claims and incompatible operational speeds

Criticism and Controversies

The Collective faces significant criticism for their business practices and social impact:

Human Rights Concerns

Critics argue that the extensive biomodifications required for Collective membership constitute a form of voluntary slavery, as members become physically dependent on maintaining high velocities to survive in their economic ecosystem.

Market Manipulation

The Archive Foundation has documented numerous instances of the Collective using their temporal advantages to engage in what traditional economics would classify as insider trading and market manipulation on an interdimensional scale.

Environmental Impact

The constant high-speed movement required by Collective operations has been linked to acceleration of local time distortions and increased frequency of temporal anomalies in their operational zones.
