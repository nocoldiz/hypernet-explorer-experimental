Mages Guild

Last updated: December 15, 2001 | 8 min read

The Mages Guild is the premier arcane organization governing magical practice, research, and enforcement in the post-Squishing world. Originally founded as the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn in 1888, the Guild was catastrophically reformed following the dimensional collapse events of 1997 and the magical resurgence of 1999.

Overview

Following the catastrophic dimensional convergence known as The Squishing, the Mages Guild emerged as the primary regulatory body for arcane activities worldwide. The organization manages spell-casting licenses, maintains the CamelCase Law enforcement protocols, and serves as the central authority for magical research and development initiatives.

Historical Foundation

The original Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn was established in 1888 by William Wynn Westcott, Samuel Liddell MacGregor Mathers, and William Robert Woodman. Operating as a secret society, the Order focused on the study and practice of ritual disciplines, hermetic traditions, and thaumaturgical research. Notable early members included A.E. Waite, Arthur Edward Waite, and Aleister Crowley himself.

The Order maintained strict hierarchical structures and initiation protocols, operating through a network of temples across Europe and North America. However, internal schisms and the outbreak of World War I severely weakened the organization, leading to its effective dissolution by 1920.

The Crowley Refounding

When the dimensional barriers collapsed in 1997 and arcane energies flooded into baseline reality during the Solomon Ritual of 1999, Aleister Crowley seized the opportunity to resurrect the fallen Order. Operating with unprecedented magical authority and backed by Lady Margaret Thatcher's political connections, Crowley launched a worldwide recruitment drive.

"The old paradigms have shattered. We must construct new frameworks for the governance of Will, the channeling of True Power, and the systematic exploitation of reality's newfound fluidity." - Aleister Crowley, Founding Charter, 1999

The reformed Guild abandoned the secretive nature of its predecessor, instead operating as a semi-public organization with government recognition and corporate sponsorship from Esoteric Heavy Industries.

Rapid Expansion Protocol

Unlike the exclusive membership policies of the original Golden Dawn, Crowley implemented aggressive recruitment protocols targeting anyone demonstrating basic thaumaturgical aptitude. This "democratization" of magical practice led to a surge in Guild membership from 200 founding members to over 50,000 registered practitioners within two years.

Organizational Structure

Supreme Council

The Guild is governed by a seven-member Supreme Council, each representing different aspects of arcane practice. Current council positions include the Archmagus of Transmutation, the Chancellor of Temporal Studies, and the Director of Dimensional Compliance.

Enforcement Division

The Guild maintains a specialized enforcement arm responsible for investigating illegal spell-casting, unauthorized dimensional breaches, and violations of the CamelCase Law. Enforcement agents are equipped with reality-stabilization equipment and counter-thaumaturgical weaponry.

Research and Development

The R&D branch focuses on systematic cataloging of post-Squishing magical phenomena, development of standardized spell-casting protocols, and integration of arcane practices with emerging technologies. Their work has led to breakthroughs in mana-battery technology and spell-automation systems.

The 92 Schools Branch

Named after the 92 dimensions that collapsed during the Squishing, this specialized division attempts to catalog and categorize the diverse magical traditions that have emerged from the dimensional convergence. Each "school" represents a distinct approach to arcane manipulation, from the crystal-focused techniques of the Venusian refugees to the probability-warping methods of the Gambler's Conclave.

Ideological Factions

Thatcher Loyalists

This faction, led by disciples of Lady Margaret Thatcher, advocates for strict regulatory control over magical practices and close cooperation with governmental authorities. They support the licensing system and believe in maintaining order through hierarchical structures and bureaucratic oversight.

Crowley Traditionalists

Following Crowley's original vision, these Guild members emphasize personal empowerment, individual will, and the exploration of consciousness through magical practice. They often clash with the Loyalists over issues of regulation and institutional control.

Corporate Technomancers

A newer faction that emerged post-1999, the Technomancers focus on integrating magical practices with digital technology and corporate methodologies. They're closely allied with Hypercapitalist Collective interests and promote the commercialization of spell-casting services.

Modern Operations and Influence

In the post-Squishing world, the Mages Guild operates as both a regulatory authority and a powerful political entity. They maintain licensing bureaus in major cities, oversee magical education programs, and coordinate with international organizations to manage cross-dimensional trade and travel.

Current Conflicts

The Guild faces ongoing tensions with several groups:

  • Open warfare with The Gods, who view the Guild as illegitimate usurpers
  • Territorial disputes with Goblin Factions over mineral rights in magically active zones
  • Ideological opposition from Archive Foundation regarding information transparency
  • Internal power struggles between the Loyalist and Traditionalist factions

Public Services

Despite political controversies, the Guild provides essential services including:

  • Spell-casting license certification and renewal
  • Standardized magical education curricula
  • Emergency response teams for magical disasters
  • Public access to basic magical research databases

As the magical landscape continues to evolve in the post-Squishing era, the Mages Guild remains a central pillar of the new world order, balancing the demands of regulation, innovation, and the fundamental human drive to harness supernatural forces for practical and personal advancement.
