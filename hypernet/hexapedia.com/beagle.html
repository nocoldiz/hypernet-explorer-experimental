Beagle

Last updated: January 15, 2001 | 7 min read

Beagle is the legendary phantom city located at the terminus of the Encompassing Road. Known as the "Place of Perditions," Beagle represents the ultimate destination for travelers who have journeyed through the dimensional highways of our post-Squishing reality. According to trucker folklore, only one person has ever returned from Beagle: the mysterious driver known as Bubba.

Dimensional Location

Beagle exists at what the Trucker Society terms a "Temporal-Velocity Gate" - the point where the Encompassing Road transitions from physical asphalt to pure conceptual pathway. Unlike conventional cities that exist in standard three-dimensional space, Beagle occupies a quantum-stabilized pocket dimension that can only be accessed by achieving critical momentum on the Road's final stretch.

The city's dimensional coordinates shift constantly, making it impossible to map using traditional navigation systems. Even advanced techno-mystical GPS units manufactured by Esoteric Heavy Industries fail to maintain lock on Beagle's position for more than 0.3 seconds.

City Characteristics

Descriptions of Beagle vary dramatically depending on the source, leading researchers to theorize that the city manifests differently for each visitor. However, several consistent elements emerge from trucker testimonies and Hypernet transmissions intercepted near the Road's terminus:

  • Architectural Fluidity: Buildings constantly reshape themselves, blending architectural styles from multiple dimensions and time periods
  • Population Paradox: The city appears both completely deserted and densely populated simultaneously
  • Temporal Distortion: Time flows in multiple directions, with some areas experiencing accelerated decay while others remain frozen
  • Gravitational Anomalies: Streets may extend vertically or curve through impossible geometries

The Perditions

The term "perditions" refers to the fate that befalls most travelers who reach Beagle. According to fragmented radio transmissions recorded by the Archive Foundation, visitors become trapped in personalized scenarios that reflect their deepest regrets, unfulfilled desires, or unresolved conflicts.

"Day 847 in Beagle. Still can't find the exit ramp. Keep seeing my old house, but it's wrong somehow. The windows are eyes and they're watching me. The GPS just keeps saying 'recalculating' but the voice sounds like my ex-wife. Send help." - Final transmission from Trucker Delta-7

The Trucker Society's theoretical department suggests that perditions function as psycho-dimensional traps, using advanced neuro-temporal algorithms to create inescapable mental loops. The city essentially becomes a prison constructed from the visitor's own consciousness.

Bubba's Return

Bubba remains the only documented individual to have reached Beagle and returned to tell about it. His account, recorded at a truck stop in former Wyoming, describes Beagle as "the place where roads go to die, but also where they're born."

According to Bubba's testimony, he encountered what he termed "the Road's Heart" - a massive engine or generator that powers the entire Encompassing Road network. He claims to have performed some form of maintenance on this device, though he refuses to elaborate on the specifics, stating only that "some things are better left under the hood."

Significantly, Bubba returned with his Liminal Engine upgraded with what he calls "Beagle protocols" - modifications that allow his vehicle to navigate dimensional boundaries with unprecedented precision.

Theoretical Framework

Several organizations have attempted to explain Beagle's nature and purpose:

Mages Guild Theory

The Mages Guild proposes that Beagle serves as a "Reality Recycling Center" where failed timelines and aborted possibilities are processed and reintegrated into the multiverse. The perditions may be a side effect of this cosmic waste management system.

Hypercapitalist Collective Theory

The Hypercapitalist Collective views Beagle as the ultimate market destination - a place where the most precious commodity (human experience) is extracted and commodified. They've attempted several hostile acquisition missions, all of which have failed catastrophically.

Archive Foundation Theory

The Archive Foundation maintains that Beagle is a glitch in the post-Squishing reality matrix - a buffer zone where unresolved data conflicts play out endlessly. They consider it a critical system component that should not be interfered with.

Safety Warnings

All major organizations strongly advise against attempting to reach Beagle. The Trucker Society has issued a permanent Code Black warning for the Road's final stretch, and travel beyond Mile Marker ∞-7 is prohibited by international treaty.

If you encounter road signs indicating proximity to Beagle, immediately engage emergency protocols:

  1. Reduce speed below 80 km/h to trigger a time loop reset
  2. Activate all available Nokia phone shields
  3. Begin broadcasting your Social Security number on CB Channel 19
  4. If possible, sacrifice a quarter tank of gasoline to the Road Gods
"Remember: Beagle isn't just a destination. It's a decision. And once you've decided to go there, you've already arrived." - Final entry in the Official Trucker's Almanac, 7th Edition
