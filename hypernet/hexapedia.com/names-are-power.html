Following the Great Reformatting, repetition of names has become a measurable source of influence-equity, transforming reputation into quantifiable power-streams.

Nomenclatural Dynamics

The Names Are Power phenomenon operates on the principle of identity-concentration through repetitive recognition. Each time a person's name is spoken, written, or digitally transmitted, it generates what researchers call "mention-flux"—a quantifiable form of influence-capital that accumulates within the named individual's personal data-matrix.

First documented by Archive Foundation researchers in early 2000, the effect appears to be a byproduct of the reality-restructuring that occurred during the Y2K Magical Emergence. The convergence of information networks with thaumic-fields created feedback loops where social recognition translates directly into measurable power.

Power Accumulation Mechanics

The accumulation process follows established info-thaumic principles:

  • Written Text: Each written instance of a name generates 1 unit of base mention-flux
  • Spoken Word: Vocal pronunciation amplifies mention-flux by 150% due to acoustic resonance-fields
  • Digital Transmission: Electronic communication adds network-effect multipliers based on reach
  • Broadcast Media: Mass media creates exponential scaling through simultaneous mention-events

Once an individual accumulates approximately 10,000 mention-flux units, they begin manifesting measurable influence-abilities. These can range from enhanced persuasion coefficients to direct reality-modification through concentrated will-projection.

Corporate Applications

The Hypercapitalist Collective was among the first to weaponize Names Are Power for market advantage. Their "reputation-mining" operations involve systematic name-propagation campaigns designed to artificially inflate executive mention-flux levels.

"We've found that a CEO with sufficient mention-flux can literally alter quarterly reports through focused intention. It's the ultimate performance enhancement." - Internal Memo, Esoteric Heavy Industries, 2001

Marketing departments now employ "name-frequency analysts" who optimize mention-distribution across multiple channels. The most successful campaigns achieve what industry insiders call "viral mention-saturation"—a critical mass where the target name becomes self-replicating through social networks.

Identity-Loss Protocols

However, the Names Are Power phenomenon carries significant risks. Excessive mention-flux accumulation can lead to several dangerous conditions:

  • Echo Syndrome: Where the individual becomes unable to distinguish between their authentic self and their public persona
  • Identity Dispersal: Consciousness becomes spread across all instances of their name, leading to fragmentary awareness
  • Mention-Lockup: Complete paralysis when mention-flux exceeds processing capacity

The Mages Guild has developed several countermeasures, including name-suppression rituals and identity-shielding techniques. However, these methods often conflict with corporate interests in maintaining high-visibility executives.

The Archive Foundation maintains strict protocols around their researchers' names, using codenames and rotating identities to prevent unauthorized mention-flux accumulation. Their internal documents refer to this as "operational name-hygiene."
