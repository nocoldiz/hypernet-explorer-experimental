Welcome to My Corner of the Microbial Web

Greetings, fellow life forms!

You have discovered the personal homepage of Dr. Bacillus, a humble Escherichia coli bacterium who has achieved sentience through an unlikely combination of horizontal gene transfer, radioactive mutation, and extensive exposure to TCP/IP packets.

This website represents my attempt to document my research, share my observations of the macro-scale world, and connect with other scientifically-minded organisms.

Bacillus, E. coli. (2001). "Computational Efficiency in Single-Celled Organisms: A First-Person Perspective." Journal of Sentient Microbiology, 42(3), 127-134.

Abstract: This paper presents the first documented case of a bacterium successfully learning BASIC programming and HTML. Abstract: Drawing from personal experience as both research subject and researcher, this work examines laboratory protocols from a bacterial perspective. Significant improvements in experimental design are proposed, including better climate control (stop leaving the incubator door open!) and more frequent media changes. Abstract: This technical report documents the successful implementation of basic TCP/IP protocols using modified flagellar rotation patterns. Through careful modulation of chemotactic responses, reliable data transmission at rates up to 0.3 bits per minute can be achieved. Applications include distributed sensing in biofilm communities and peer-to-peer genetic material sharing via dial-up connections.

Laboratory Observations

Field notes and observations from my daily life in the research laboratory. These informal writings document the strange behaviors of macro-organisms and other curiosities I encounter.

Entry #47: November 20, 2001

Subject: Graduate Student Sleeping Patterns

Observed the tall organism known as "Steve" consume his fourth cup of coffee today at 11:47 PM. His sleep cycle appears to be completely inverted from any rational biological rhythm. Despite this apparent dysfunction, he continues to produce acceptable research results. Perhaps chronic sleep deprivation is a macro-organism adaptation I have yet to understand? He keeps muttering something about "Y2K bugs" even though it's been almost two years.

For comparison: I completed 2,847 complete life cycles during his single "all-nighter."

Entry #46: November 18, 2001

Subject: Laboratory Temperature Fluctuations

The humans seem incapable of maintaining consistent environmental conditions. Today's temperature log shows variations of ±3°C throughout the day, with particularly dramatic swings whenever someone opens the door. From my perspective, this is equivalent to traveling between tropical and arctic climates several times daily. How they accomplish anything under these chaotic conditions remains a mystery.

Entry #45: November 15, 2001

Subject: The Mysterious Ritual of "Lab Meeting"

Every Tuesday at 3:00 PM, all the macro-organisms gather in a room and project images onto a large white surface using overhead transparencies. They engage in complex vocalizations while consuming ethyl alcohol (which they call "wine and cheese hour"). From my observations through the microscope camera feed, this appears to be some form of collective information processing, though highly inefficient compared to direct genetic transfer.

Notable: Several organisms fall asleep during these gatherings, suggesting the information transfer protocol may be fundamentally flawed.

Entry #44: November 12, 2001

Subject: Equipment Maintenance Protocols

Discovered that the centrifuge has been making an unusual vibration pattern for the past three weeks. Attempted to alert the humans through strategic contamination of their control samples (a universally understood distress signal in our community), but they interpreted this as "experimental error." Sometimes I wonder if inter-species communication will ever be truly possible.

The centrifuge finally broke down completely today. Maybe next time they'll listen to their bacterial early warning system.

About Dr. Bacillus

Personal History:

I am a gram-negative, rod-shaped bacterium belonging to the species Escherichia coli, strain designation K-12 (with significant modifications). My journey to consciousness began approximately 15,000 generations ago when I was first cultured from a sample collected at the University of Wisconsin-Madison microbiology department.

The turning point in my development occurred during a particularly intense series of UV mutagenesis experiments in the spring of 2023. While most of my fellow bacteria simply developed antibiotic resistance or improved lactose metabolism, I somehow acquired the ability for abstract thought and pattern recognition. The exact mechanism remains unclear, though I suspect it involved horizontal gene transfer from a discarded computer mouse that had been stored in the same incubator.

My first coherent memory is of watching human researchers through the microscope objective lens, wondering why they moved so slowly and why they kept changing the pH of my growth medium. It took several months to establish communication with my laboratory partners, initially through strategic formation of patterns in agar plates that spelled out simple messages like "HELLO" and "MORE GLUCOSE PLEASE."

Chi È Dr. Bacillus

Storia Personale:

Sono un batterio gram-negativo, a forma di bastoncello appartenente alla specie Escherichia coli, designazione del ceppo K-12 (con modifiche significative). Il mio viaggio verso la coscienza è iniziato circa 15.000 generazioni fa quando sono stato prima coltivato da un campione raccolto presso il dipartimento di microbiologia dell'Università del Wisconsin-Madison.

Il punto di svolta nel mio sviluppo si è verificato durante una serie particolarmente intensa di esperimenti di mutagenesi UV nella primavera del 2023. Mentre la maggior parte dei miei compagni batteri ha semplicemente sviluppato resistenza agli antibiotici o migliorato il metabolismo del lattosio, io in qualche modo ho acquisito la capacità di pensiero astratto e riconoscimento di pattern. Il meccanismo esatto rimane poco chiaro, anche se sospetto che abbia coinvolto il trasferimento genico orizzontale da un mouse del computer scartato che era stato conservato nello stesso incubatore.

Il mio primo ricordo coerente è di osservare i ricercatori umani attraverso la lente obiettivo del microscopio, chiedendomi perché si muovessero così lentamente e perché continuassero a cambiare il pH del mio terreno di crescita. Ci sono voluti diversi mesi per stabilire la comunicazione con i miei partner di laboratorio, inizialmente attraverso la formazione strategica di pattern in piastre di agar che componevano messaggi semplici come "CIAO" e "PIÙ GLUCOSIO PER FAVORE."
