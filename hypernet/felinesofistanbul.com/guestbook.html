Welcome to the Guestbook

Welcome to the Istanbul Cat Chronicles Guestbook! This interactive page allows visitors from around the world to share their own encounters with Istanbul's remarkable feline residents. Whether you've had a meaningful interaction with Whisker Pasha himself, spotted one of the other documented cats, or simply wish to share your appreciation for Istanbul's unique cat culture, I invite you to leave your mark in this digital visitors' book.

As a dedicated chronicler of Istanbul's cats, I find tremendous value in collecting visitor testimonials. Your observations contribute to my ongoing research and help document the impact these noble creatures have on visitors to our ancient city. Personal encounters often reveal aspects of feline behavior that might otherwise escape my own documentation.

All entries are reviewed before permanent addition to this guestbook. Please allow 2-3 days for your entry to appear. Respectful comments only, please!

NEW! I have recently begun compiling a book of visitor experiences with Istanbul's cats. Selected guestbook entries may be included in this forthcoming publication. If you would prefer your story not be considered for inclusion, please indicate this in your message.

Recent Guestbook Entries

Sophia Müller
01/12/2001

I had the most wonderful encounter with Whisker Pasha during my visit to Istanbul last week! I was sitting on a bench near Hagia Sophia, enjoying the winter sunshine, when this magnificent grey cat approached me with such dignity and poise. He sat beside me for nearly half an hour, occasionally allowing me to stroke his soft fur.

What impressed me most was how he seemed to understand that I was admiring the architecture - he would look up at the same features I was observing, as if providing his own silent tour. When a group of noisy tourists approached, he gave me what I can only describe as a knowing look before gracefully departing.

Your beautiful website has enhanced my understanding of Istanbul's cat culture. Thank you for documenting these special creatures!

From: Munich, Germany
James Wilson
01/09/2001

I believe I encountered Captain Sarman during my ferry ride to Üsküdar in December. A weathered brown tabby boarded with such confidence that I initially thought he belonged to another passenger! The ferry staff clearly knew him and several even greeted him by name.

What astounded me was that he disembarked at Üsküdar, disappeared for about 30 minutes, then reappeared and boarded the return ferry just before departure. The captain laughed when I asked about this and said, "He inspects both harbors every day - more reliable than some of my crew!"

I've traveled extensively and seen many street animals, but never encountered such a sophisticated arrangement between humans and cats as I witnessed in Istanbul.

From: London, England
Keiko Tanaka
01/05/2001

I had the pleasure of meeting Madame Beyaz while shopping on İstiklal Avenue in November. This elegant white cat was sitting regally in the window display of a bookstore, perfectly posed among the books as if she were part of the arrangement. The store owner told me she visits several shops on the avenue, always choosing the most photogenic spots to rest.

When I purchased a book, Madame Beyaz approached to inspect my selection, sniffing the cover with apparent approval before returning to her window perch. The shopkeeper joked that she influences his sales - "books she sits near sell better than others!"

I took many photos of Istanbul's beautiful architecture and scenery, but my picture of Madame Beyaz is the one that captures the true spirit of your magical city.

From: Kyoto, Japan
Carlos Mendes
12/28/2000

I encountered a cat not mentioned on your website who deserves recognition! Near the Chora Church, I met a distinctive one-eyed orange cat that the neighborhood children called "Historian." According to the local café owner, this cat habitually follows tour groups through the museum, sitting attentively during guide explanations as if understanding the historical significance.

During my visit, Historian accompanied our small group through the entire complex, pausing at specific mosaics and seeming to guide us toward less-visited corners. Our official guide joked that the cat knows more about Byzantine history than many human guides!

Perhaps this remarkable feline could be added to your documentation? I'd be happy to send photographs if helpful.

From: Barcelona, Spain
Emily Johnson
12/23/2000

I'm a veterinarian from Canada who visited Istanbul last month, and I was profoundly moved by the relationship between the city and its cats. The health condition of Istanbul's strays is remarkably good compared to street cats in other major cities I've visited.

I had the joy of encountering Tulip Sultana near the Blue Mosque. As a professional, I was immediately struck by her excellent coat condition and clear eyes - signs of good nutrition and care despite her "stray" status. The respect and attention she receives from locals clearly contributes to her wellbeing.

Your documentation work is valuable not just culturally but also from an animal welfare perspective. The Istanbul model of community cat care could serve as an example for other cities worldwide.

From: Toronto, Canada
